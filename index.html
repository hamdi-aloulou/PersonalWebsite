About Me

I'm a postdoctoral researcher at "Montpellier Laboratory of Informatics, Robotics and Microelectronics" (LIRMM, CNRS URM 5506), within the "Interactive Digital Humans" (IDH) team. As part of my postdoctoral work, I'm actively involved in the future investment project “VHP Inter@ctive” and the “QoL Chair” (Dependent Persons Quality of Life Chair) of the Institute Mines Telecom. My research interests are focused on the semantic modeling and reasoning for context awareness based on augmented sensing information. This work is applied to the development of intelligent living spaces.

Before joining LIRMM, I was attached to the French-Singaporean international joint laboratory “Image and Pervasive Access Lab” (IPAL CNRS UMI 2955) for three years working within the “Pervasive Access and Wellbeing Management” (PAWM) team. During this period, I have carried out my PhD entitled “Framework for Ambient Assistive Living: Handling Dynamism and Uncertainty in Real Time Semantic Services Provisioning” under the supervision of Prof. “Mounir Mokhtari”. Over my thesis, I developed a dynamic and extensible framework for ambient assistive living, able to adapt to different environments and the needs of patients. This was realized based on the semantic plug&play approach I have proposed. I have also been working on uncertain information in smart environments and I have proposed an approach for uncertainty measurement based on sensors intrinsic characteristics and their functional comportment.

Research Projects

VHP Inter@ctive - Future investments

A large scale deployment of assistive solution

The ambition of VHP-inter@ctive is to reinforce the care on the life place, at home or in a nursing home (EHPAD - Etablissement d'Hebergement pour Personnes Agees Dependantes), of the people with chronic diseases (Focusing around the diabetes type 2 pathology), in their education, the risk prevention and their healthcare, due to innovative teleservices using different ICT solutions.

Chair Quality of Life

Created in January 2011, this chair aims to experiment and validate on large scale the concept of smart homes. Supported by the French Institute of Telecommunications, French Telecom Foundation and two French health care plans "Mutuelle Generale" and "Reunica", the chair is interested by ageing people suffering from cognitive and physical problems.

AMUPADH - Activity Monitoring and UI Plasticity for supporting Ageing with Mild Dementia at Home

The project addresses the improvement of QoL (Quality of Life) for the elderly, especially those with dementia, by enabling their home with ambient assistive living technologies. It includes a real life deployment of the proposed system in a collaborating nursing home in Singapore.
